Frequently Asked Questions

Generally , Many people have questions related to mental health. Here are some common questions and their answers.

  • Why mental health is important?

    It affects how we think, feel, and act as we cope with life. It also helps determine how we handle stress, relate to others, and make choices. Mental health is important at every stage of life, from childhood and adolescence through adulthood and aging..

  • I feel quite low sometimes. Should I see a therapist?

    Most people feel more comfortable confiding in a close friend or family member in the first instance. Often simply talking about it helps. If This is not possible then easiest way is to use virtual mental health assistant for the quick , non judgemental reply to you

  • What causes depression?

    We all have days when we feel down, but those feelings usually pass without having too much impact on our lives. But if they last beyond a couple of weeks or you feel as though things are getting worse, it could be a sign that you’re experiencing depression.

  • What is a personality disorder?

    If you have been diagnosed with a personality disorder it doesn’t mean that you’re fundamentally different from anyone else, but at times you might need extra support.

  • What is the best way to improve my mental wellbeing?

    We all have mental health to look after, as well as physical health, and the way we live our lives has a direct influence on them both. There are lots of small things we can change to improve or maintain good mental health and wellbeing without spending lots of money to do it.
